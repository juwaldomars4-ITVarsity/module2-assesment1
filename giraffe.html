Giraffe

DescriptionGiraffes are the tallest of the land animals. It is a hooved mammal with short, wiry hair that varies in colors among the various races. The background coloring is cream with borders of gold to orange and the splotches are brown to black.SizeHeight (to top of head): newborns average 1.8 m (6 ft.); adults range 4.25 to 5.5 m (14 to 18 ft.) Male: Up to 6.2 m (18.6 ft.) Female: Up to 5.3 m (15.7 ft.)WeightMale: 1,100 to 1,915 kg (2,420 to 4,215 lbs.) Female: 700 to 1,181 kg (1,540 to 2,600 lbs.)DietSelective browsers that prefer new growth foliage of acacia, mimosa brush, and Combretum trees 2 to 5 m (6.6 to 16.5 ft.) high; feed on shoots & leaves of 100+ plant species, including some herbs; choice determined by seasonal and local availabilityIncubationApproximately 15 months (450 to 465 days); typically one offspring at a timeSexual MaturityMale: Approximately 3.5 years; usually will not breed until at least 8 years of age Female: Approximately 4 to 5 yearsLife Span20 to 25 years, 28 year approximate maxRangeAfrican regions south of the Sahara DesertHabitatSavannah, bush, scrub, and open acacia woodlands; semi-arid regions to regions of dense vegetationPopulationGlobal: UnknownStatus IUCN: Lower Risk/Conservation Dependent CITES: Not listed USFWS: Not listed Currently not protected except within national parks
